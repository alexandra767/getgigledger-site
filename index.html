Auto Mileage Tracking

Automatic GPS trip detection with jitter filtering and signal-loss recovery. Every trip is valued at the IRS rate on its date — $0.725/mi Jan–Jun, $0.76/mi Jul–Dec 2026 — and commute miles are auto-split from delivery miles.

Multi-Platform Earnings

Log income from DoorDash, Uber, Instacart, Lyft, Grubhub, and any 1099 platform. Filter by platform and month with collapsible daily breakdowns.

Screenshot Import (OCR)

Snap your earnings screen from DoorDash, Uber Eats, Instacart, Grubhub, or Amazon Flex. OCR detects the platform and extracts pay, tips, deliveries, and time.

Real-Time Tax Estimates

Self-employment, federal, state, and local tax calculated after every dash. Know exactly what to set aside — no April surprises.

Schedule C & Tax Packet

A live Schedule C preview organized by IRS line item, plus a complete accountant-ready packet: mileage log, expenses, 1099s, and quarterly payments. Export as PDF or TurboTax CSV.

Expense & Fuel Tracking

Scan gas receipts to create fuel entries with MPG and cost-per-mile. Log phone bills, insurance, and repairs — or set them up once as recurring expenses.

Apple Watch Companion

Live miles, GPS signal strength, delivery count, and daily goal progress on your wrist. Tap “Delivery Done” with per-platform tagging — phone stays in the mount.

Everything gig workers need to know.

GigLedger offers a 7-day free trial with full access to every feature. After the trial, choose Monthly ($4.99/mo) or Yearly ($39.99/yr — save 33%). No ads, no data selling, and no hidden fees. All features are included with both plans.
GigLedger supports all major gig platforms including DoorDash, Uber, Uber Eats, Lyft, Instacart, Grubhub, Amazon Flex, Spark, and more. You can also add custom platforms for any 1099 contract work.
GigLedger offers GPS-based automatic trip tracking that records your route, distance, and calculates your IRS standard mileage deduction at the rate in force on each trip's date. The IRS raised the 2026 rate mid-year (Announcement 2026-11): $0.725/mile from January 1 to June 30 and $0.76/mile from July 1 onward — GigLedger applies the correct rate to every trip automatically, so your Schedule C stays accurate through the change. It uses GPS jitter filtering to eliminate phantom distance from parking lots, signal-loss recovery to keep tracking through tunnels and garages, and automatically splits commute miles from delivery miles. It detects delivery stops, splits multi-stop trips, works in the background, and survives app kills. You can also log trips manually.
All data is stored locally on your device. On iOS, data is stored using Apple's SwiftData framework with optional iCloud sync via your personal Apple account. On Android, data is stored using Room (SQLite). Nothing is sent to external servers. There is no tracking, no ads, and no third-party data sharing.
GigLedger generates a Schedule C preview PDF organized by IRS line items that you can share with your tax preparer. It calculates estimated self-employment tax, federal, state, and local taxes in real time, and reminds you of quarterly payment deadlines.
Most gig workers should set aside 25-30% of their gross earnings for taxes. This covers self-employment tax (15.3%), federal income tax, and state/local taxes. GigLedger calculates your exact set-aside amount after every dash based on your actual tax situation.

If you expect to owe more than $1,000 in taxes for the year, the IRS requires you to make quarterly estimated payments (due in April, June, September, and January). GigLedger tracks your quarterly liability and sends reminders before each deadline so you never miss a payment.
